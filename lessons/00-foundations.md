# Lesson 00 — Foundations (Read This First)

The **Deconstruction** step. These patterns turn ~180 words into thousands of sentences.
Spend a day here before Day 01 — everything else plugs into this. Start with §0.0: it
x-rays how Italian assembles, so the rest is just filling in the slots.

## 0.0 The Deconstruction Sentences — the grammar x-ray
Tim Ferriss' core trick: a handful of sentences that expose a language's whole machinery.
Translate these ten, understand *why* each works, and you've mapped ~80% of Italian sentence
structure. Re-derive them from memory once a week.

| Italian | English | What it reveals |
|---|---|---|
| La mela è rossa. | The apple is red. | Adjective *follows* the noun and agrees (mela = fem → ross**a**). |
| È la mela di Marco. | It's Marco's apple. | Possession = **di** + owner (no 's). |
| Do la mela a Marco. | I give the apple to Marco. | Subject dropped (*do* = I give); indirect object marked by **a**. |
| Gli do la mela. | I give him the apple. | Indirect pronoun **gli** goes *before* the verb. |
| Gliela do. | I give it to him. | Two pronouns merge (gli + la → **gliela**) and stack before the verb. |
| Devo dargliela. | I have to give it to him. | Modal (*devo*) + infinitive; pronouns **attach** to the infinitive. |
| Posso / Voglio dargliela. | I can / want to give it to him. | Same frame for *potere* & *volere*. |
| Non posso mangiarla. | I can't eat it. | Negation = **non** before the verb; pronoun on the infinitive. |
| Le ho dato la mela. | I gave her the apple. | Past = *avere* + participle; pronoun sits before *ho*. |
| Quando me la dai? | When are you giving it to me? | Question by intonation only; **mi → me** before another pronoun. |

> 🔑 Everything below (§0.1–0.10) is the detail that makes these ten sentences click.

## 0.1 Pronunciation — say it right from day one
Italian is phonetic once you know ~8 rules.

| Letter / combo | Sound | Example |
|---|---|---|
| c + e/i | "ch" (church) | ciao, cena |
| c + a/o/u, or ch | hard "k" | casa, che, chi |
| g + e/i | soft "j" (jam) | gelato, giorno |
| g + a/o/u, or gh | hard "g" (go) | gatto, spaghetti |
| gli | "lli" (million) | famiglia, figlio |
| gn | "ny" (canyon) | gnocchi, signore |
| sc + e/i | "sh" (ship) | pesce, sciare |
| sc + a/o/u/h | "sk" | scuola, scherzo |
| z | "ts" / "dz" | grazie, zero |
| h | always silent | ho, hanno |
| r | rolled / trilled | Roma, caro |
| double consonant | held longer (meaning changes) | nonno ≠ nono, pizza |
| a e i o u | pure vowels, never glide | a=ah e=eh i=ee o=oh u=oo |

**Stress** usually = second-to-last syllable (par-LA-re). A written accent moves it to the final vowel: *caffè, città, perché, più*.

## 0.2 Articles, gender & agreement
Every noun is masculine or feminine; articles and adjectives must match.

**The (definite):** il (most masc) · lo (masc before z, s+cons, gn, ps) · l' (before vowel) ·
la (fem) · i / gli (masc plural) · le (fem plural)

**A/an (indefinite):** un (masc) · uno (masc before z, s+cons…) · una (fem) · un' (fem before vowel)

**Gender by ending:** -o → masc (plural -i: libro→libri) · -a → fem (plural -e: casa→case) ·
-e → either (plural -i: cane→cani)

**Agreement:** *un libro nuovo → due libri nuovi* · *una casa nuova → due case nuove* ·
-e adjectives change only for number: *un cane grande → due cani grandi*.

## 0.3 Verb patterns — three endings unlock hundreds
Drop -are/-ere/-ire, add the endings. Pronouns are usually dropped — the ending says who.

| | -ARE (parlare) | -ERE (prendere) | -IRE (dormire) |
|---|---|---|---|
| io | parlo | prendo | dormo |
| tu | parli | prendi | dormi |
| lui/lei | parla | prende | dorme |
| noi | parliamo | prendiamo | dormiamo |
| voi | parlate | prendete | dormite |
| loro | parlano | prendono | dormono |

*-isc- verbs:* capire → capisco, capisci, capisce, capiamo, capite, capiscono (also finire, preferire).

**Past (passato prossimo)** = present of *avere*/*essere* + participle (-ato/-uto/-ito):
*Ho mangiato la pizza.* · movement/change verbs use *essere* and agree: *Sono andato/andata a casa.*
Irregulars: fare→fatto, dire→detto, vedere→visto, prendere→preso, essere/stare→stato.

## 0.4 Numbers to 100 + prices
Tens: 10 dieci · 20 venti · 30 trenta · 40 quaranta · 50 cinquanta · 60 sessanta ·
70 settanta · 80 ottanta · 90 novanta · 100 cento.

Build by snapping the unit on; drop the ten's final vowel before *uno* and *otto*:
*ventuno, ventidue, ventitré, trentotto, quarantasei, novantanove*.

Prices: *Quanto costa? · Costa dieci euro. · Sono venticinque euro. · Tre euro e cinquanta (€3.50).*

## 0.5 Cognate rules — you already know thousands of words
English and Italian share Latin roots. Swap the ending and most "big" words appear for free.

| English ending | Italian | Example |
|---|---|---|
| -tion | **-zione** | information → informazione |
| -ty | **-tà** | city → città · university → università |
| -ble | **-bile** | possible → possibile |
| -ous | **-oso** | famous → famoso |
| -ly | **-mente** | normally → normalmente |
| -ant / -ent | **-ante / -ente** | important → importante |
| -ism / -ist | **-ismo / -ista** | tourism → turismo · artist → artista |

> ⚠️ **False friends:** *parente* = relative (not parent) · *libreria* = bookshop (*biblioteca* = library) ·
> *caldo* = hot (not cold) · *morbido* = soft (not morbid) · *fattoria* = farm (not factory).

## 0.6 Object pronouns & where they go
| Type | me | you | him/her/it | us | you (pl) | them |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Direct** (it/him) | mi | ti | **lo / la** | ci | vi | **li / le** |
| **Indirect** (to…) | mi | ti | **gli / le** | ci | vi | gli |

- **Before** a conjugated verb: *Lo vedo.* (I see it/him) · *Ti chiamo.* (I call you)
- **Attached** to an infinitive: *Voglio veder**lo**.* (I want to see it) — with modals both work: *Lo voglio vedere = Voglio vederlo.*
- **Stacked** (indirect + direct): mi/ti/ci/vi → me/te/ce/ve, and gli/le → glie- :
  *Me lo dai?* (Will you give it to me?) · *Glielo do.* (I give it to him/her.)

## 0.7 Modal + infinitive — the request engine
*volere / potere / dovere* + an **infinitive** builds hundreds of sentences:
*Voglio mangiare.* (I want to eat) · *Posso pagare?* (Can I pay?) · *Devo andare.* (I have to go).
Polite forms you'll lean on: **Vorrei…** (I'd like…) · **Potrei…?** (Could I…?) —
*Vorrei un caffè. Potrei avere il conto?*

## 0.8 Preposition + article contractions
Five prepositions fuse with the article. Learn the pattern, not 30 words.

| + | il | la | l' | i | gli | le |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **di** | del | della | dell' | dei | degli | delle |
| **a** | al | alla | all' | ai | agli | alle |
| **da** | dal | dalla | dall' | dai | dagli | dalle |
| **in** | nel | nella | nell' | nei | negli | nelle |
| **su** | sul | sulla | sull' | sui | sugli | sulle |

*la chiave **della** porta* (the key of the door) · *vado **al** lavoro* (I go to work) · *sono **nel** negozio* (I'm in the shop).
*con* and *per* don't contract.

## 0.9 Questions, negation & "there is"
- **Questions:** no "do", no inversion — just raise your pitch. *Parli inglese?* (Do you speak English?)
  Add a question word in front: *chi, (che) cosa, dove, quando, perché, come, quanto.* — *Dove abiti? Quanto costa?*
- **Negation:** put **non** before the verb — *Non capisco.* Double negatives are correct:
  *Non c'è nessuno.* (There's no one.) · *Non ho mai visto niente.*
- **There is / are:** **c'è** (sing.) · **ci sono** (plur.) — *C'è un problema. Ci sono due persone.*

## 0.10 Possessives & essere vs stare
- **Possessives** take the article: *il mio / la mia / i miei / le mie*; then *tuo, suo* (his/her/your-formal), *nostro, vostro, loro.*
  *la mia casa, il tuo numero.* Drop the article with one family member: *mia madre, tuo fratello.*
- **essere vs stare** (both ≈ "to be"): *essere* = identity / permanent (*Sono australiano. È difficile.*);
  *stare* = state / health / location / -ing (*Come stai? Sto bene. Sto mangiando.* = I'm eating).
