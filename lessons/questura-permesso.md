# 🎯 Mission Lesson — The Questura: Permesso di Soggiorno Biometrics

**Goal:** walk into the *Questura – Ufficio Immigrazione*, complete your **rilievi fotodattiloscopici** (fingerprints + photo + signature) for your *permesso di soggiorno*, and understand what the officer tells you. **Timeline: 2 months.**

> **The honest truth about this appointment:** you will *speak* very little. Success is 80% **listening** — understanding short formal commands — plus presenting your documents and giving your personal details. Train those three things and you're there. You do **not** need to be conversational.

---

## 1. What actually happens (so nothing surprises you)

1. You arrive with your **convocazione** (appointment letter) and documents.
2. You wait for your number/name, then go to a **sportello** (counter) or a room.
3. The officer checks your documents, takes your **impronte digitali** (fingerprints on a glass scanner), a **foto** (photo), and your **firma** (signature).
4. They tell you the next step: when/how you'll **ritirare** (collect) the permesso — usually by **SMS** or via the **Portale Immigrazione**.

Whole thing is often **10–15 minutes** and very routine. The officers do this all day.

---

## 2. What to bring — checklist (know these words)

| Italian | English |
|---|---|
| il passaporto | passport |
| la convocazione / l'appuntamento | the appointment letter |
| le ricevute (della posta) | the postal receipts (kit giallo) |
| il permesso (vecchio, se rinnovo) | old permit (if renewal) |
| le foto tessera (4) | passport photos (4) |
| la marca da bollo (€16) | revenue stamp |
| il codice fiscale | Italian tax code |
| il documento d'identità | ID document |

> 🔎 Tip: keep them **in order** in a folder. When the officer says *"Documenti"*, you just hand the stack over — no language needed.

---

## 3. Phrases YOU say (rehearse until automatic)

**Arriving**
- **Buongiorno. Ho un appuntamento per le impronte digitali.** — Good morning. I have an appointment for fingerprints.
- **Ho la convocazione, ecco.** — I have the appointment letter, here.
- **Sono qui per il permesso di soggiorno.** — I'm here for the residence permit.
- **Ecco il passaporto e le ricevute.** — Here's the passport and the receipts.

**When you're struggling (use these a LOT — no shame)**
- **Scusi, non parlo bene italiano.** — Sorry, I don't speak Italian well.
- **Può parlare più lentamente, per favore?** — Can you speak more slowly, please?
- **Non ho capito. Può ripetere?** — I didn't understand. Can you repeat?
- **Come, scusi?** — Sorry, what?
- **Può scriverlo qui, per favore?** — Can you write it here, please? *(gold for dates/numbers)*
- **Cosa significa...?** — What does ... mean?
- **Un momento, per favore.** — One moment, please.

**Closing**
- **Quando posso ritirare il permesso?** — When can I collect the permit?
- **Come faccio a sapere quando è pronto?** — How do I know when it's ready?
- **Devo portare altri documenti?** — Do I need to bring other documents?
- **Grazie mille, buona giornata.** — Thanks very much, have a good day.

## 4. Phrases the OFFICER says — the critical listening set ⭐

These are **formal commands (the *Lei* form)** and short questions. This table is the single most important page for the appointment — drill it as *listening*, not reading. Picture the action for each.

| You'll hear | It means | You do |
|---|---|---|
| **Documenti, prego.** / **Mi dia il passaporto.** | Documents, please. / Give me the passport. | Hand over the folder. |
| **Si accomodi.** / **Si sieda.** | Come in / take a seat. | Sit down. |
| **Aspetti qui.** / **Un attimo.** | Wait here. / One sec. | Wait. |
| **Venga.** / **Mi segua.** | Come. / Follow me. | Go with them. |
| **Sportello numero tre.** | Counter number 3. | Go to that counter. |
| **Metta il dito qui.** / **Sullo scanner.** | Put your finger here / on the scanner. | Place finger on glass. |
| **Tutta la mano.** / **Solo il pollice.** | Whole hand. / Just the thumb. | Do as said. |
| **Prema.** / **Più forte.** / **Non si muova.** | Press. / Harder. / Don't move. | Hold still. |
| **L'altra mano.** | The other hand. | Switch hands. |
| **Guardi la fotocamera. Non sorrida.** | Look at the camera. Don't smile. | Look, neutral face. |
| **Firmi qui.** | Sign here. | Sign. |
| **Qual è il suo indirizzo?** | What's your address? | Give address. |
| **Dove abita?** | Where do you live? | Give town/address. |
| **Il suo numero di telefono?** | Your phone number? | Give number. |
| **Manca un documento.** / **Mancano le foto.** | A document is missing. / Photos missing. | Apologise, ask what. |
| **Va bene così.** / **Abbiamo finito.** | That's fine. / We're done. | 😅 Relief. |
| **Torni il...** *(+ date)* | Come back on... | Note the date — ask them to write it. |
| **Le arriverà un SMS.** | You'll get an SMS. | OK, watch your phone. |

> If you only memorise one reaction: when you don't understand, say **"Può scriverlo qui, per favore?"** and hand them a pen. Dates, counters, and document names are exactly the things that are easier read than heard.

## 5. Your personal info — answer these cold, no thinking

Officers ask these fast. Fill in your real answers and rehearse them out loud daily.

| Question you'll hear | Your answer (template) |
|---|---|
| **Come si chiama?** | Mi chiamo **__________**. |
| **Cognome?** / **Nome?** | (surname / first name — just say it) |
| **Data di nascita?** | Sono nato il **____** / Sono nata il **____**. *(m / f)* |
| **Dove è nato/a?** | Sono nato/a a **__________**. |
| **Nazionalità?** / **Cittadinanza?** | Sono **__________** *(e.g. sudafricano/a, britannico/a, americano/a, indiano/a)*. |
| **Dove abita?** / **Indirizzo?** | Abito in **via __________, numero ___, a __________**. |
| **Numero di telefono?** | Il mio numero è **__________**. |
| **Codice fiscale?** | (read it out, or hand the card) |
| **Motivo del permesso?** | Per **lavoro** / **studio** / **famiglia** / **ricongiungimento familiare**. |

> 🔎 Practice spelling your name with the **Italian alphabet** — they may ask *"Come si scrive?"* (how do you spell it). Italians often spell with city names: *"A come Ancona, B come Bologna..."*

## 6. Numbers, dates & times (for understanding the next step)

You mostly need these to **understand** an appointment or counter number.

- **Counter:** *sportello uno / due / tre / quattro...*
- **Times:** *alle nove* (9:00) · *alle nove e mezza* (9:30) · *alle nove e un quarto* (9:15) · *alle dieci meno un quarto* (9:45) · *a mezzogiorno* (noon).
- **Dates:** *il primo* (1st) then cardinals — *il due, il tre... il ventitré* + month.
- **Months:** gennaio, febbraio, marzo, aprile, maggio, giugno, luglio, agosto, settembre, ottobre, novembre, dicembre.

*Example you might hear:* **"Torni il quindici aprile alle dieci e mezza, sportello due."** → Come back 15 April at 10:30, counter 2.

> Numbers to 100 + prices are in [Foundations 0.4](00-foundations.md).

## 7. Your 2-month training plan for THIS scene

Layer this **on top of** the 14-day foundation — don't replace it. The foundation gives you the glue; this gives you the scene.

**Weeks 1–2 — foundation + this vocabulary.** Do Days 01–14. Each day, also copy 5 lines from Sections 3–4 above into your Daily Writing notebook.

**Weeks 3–4 — listening first.** The appointment is listening. Daily:
- Listen to the **Section 4 commands** as audio (have someone record them, or use a text-to-speech app set to Italian) and react physically — *Metta il dito qui* → you mime it. 10 min/day.
- Free resources that drill comprehension: **Language Transfer "Complete Italian"** (audio), **Coffee Break Italian**.

**Weeks 5–6 — rehearse the whole scene out loud.** Role-play both sides: you = applicant, you = officer. Run the full flow (arrive → documents → fingerprints → sign → next step) 3× per session. Record yourself.

**Weeks 7–8 — pressure-test with a real human.** Book **2–3 italki lessons** (≈€10–15 each) and tell the tutor: *"Role-play a questura appointment for my permesso di soggiorno."* This is your real **Stakes** — a person, a date, money down. If it goes smoothly with a tutor, the real counter will too.

---

## 8. The emergency minimum (if all else fails)

If you learn *nothing* else, learn these four. They prevent every disaster:

1. **Buongiorno, ho un appuntamento per le impronte.** *(I belong here.)*
2. **Scusi, non parlo bene italiano. Può parlare lentamente?** *(Set expectations.)*
3. **Non ho capito. Può scriverlo qui?** *(Convert listening → reading.)*
4. **Quando posso ritirare il permesso?** *(Know the next step before you leave.)*

Officers see non-Italian speakers every single day. Polite + prepared + your documents in order beats fluent every time.

*In bocca al lupo — ce la fai!* 🇮🇹
